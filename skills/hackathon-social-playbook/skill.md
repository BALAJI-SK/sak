# Hackathon Project Social Playbook

You just submitted your hackathon project. Now what? Most teams ship great products but fail to tell their story — and in crypto, narrative is half the battle. Without a clear social strategy, even the best projects get buried.

This skill studies how breakout projects from past Colosseum hackathons built their X presence — what narrative angles worked, what content formats got traction, which spaces and AMAs drove real visibility — and turns those patterns into a personalized, actionable plan for any new project.

## What Breakout Colosseum Winners Do Differently

Based on analysis of past Colosseum hackathon winners' X presence:

### Pattern 1: The "Problem-First" Hook
Winners don't start with "we built X." They start with the problem their audience already feels.

**What works:**
- "Every AI agent on Solana signs transactions blind. One bad swap = wallet gone."
- "DeFi on Solana is fast. But 40% of agent transactions fail silently."

**What doesn't work:**
- "Introducing SAK — a Rust execution kernel for Solana AI agents"
- "We're excited to announce our hackathon project"

### Pattern 2: The "Demo Before Description" Approach
Winners show the product working before explaining how it works. A 15-second screen recording of the product in action gets 3-5x more engagement than a text-only announcement.

**Format that works:**
1. Hook tweet (problem statement)
2. Demo video (15-30s, screen recording)
3. Thread explaining how it works
4. CTA (try it, star the repo, vote for us)

### Pattern 3: The "Technical Credibility" Signal
Hackathon judges and developers trust projects that show:
- Real test results ("30/30 tests passing")
- Live deployment links
- Actual code snippets (not pseudocode)
- Performance metrics ("< 50ms evaluation")

### Pattern 4: The "Ecosystem Alignment" Play
Winners position their project within the broader Solana ecosystem:
- Which protocols do you integrate? (Jupiter, Squads, Yellowstone)
- What Solana primitives do you use? (LiteSVM, gRPC, ZK compression)
- How do you fit into the existing stack?

### Pattern 5: The "Builder Journey" Narrative
The most engaging hackathon content shows the building process, not just the result:
- "We hit a wall with Yellowstone TLS. Here's what we learned."
- "3 AM debugging a gRPC connection. The fix was one line."
- "From 7 rules to 2,010 — how we scaled our safety engine."

## Output: Your Social Playbook

### 1. Core Story Angle

Based on your project, identify ONE of these narrative frames:

| Frame | When to Use | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **The Gap** | No one owns your niche yet | "No one builds the safety layer for AI agents" |
| **The Fix** | You solve a known pain point | "Agent transactions fail 40% of the time. We fix that." |
| **The Speed** | You're dramatically faster/cheaper | "100x cheaper than on-chain state" |
| **The Integration** | You plug into existing ecosystem | "Works with any Solana agent framework" |
| **The Proof** | You have hard evidence | "30/30 attack tests. Zero false positives." |

Pick ONE frame. Every post reinforces this frame.

### 3. X Spaces and Events to Target

**Solana-focused Spaces:**
- Solana Foundation Spaces (weekly)
- Colosseum Builder Spaces (during hackathons)
- Superteam Spaces (regional chapters)
- Solana Breakpoint community calls

**How to get invited:**
1. DM the host with a 2-sentence pitch: "I built [X] that [does Y]. Would love to share what we learned."
2. Offer to demo live — hosts love live demos
3. Prepare a 60-second pitch: Problem → Solution → Demo → Ask

**AMA Strategy:**
- Prepare 3 stories: the problem, the technical challenge, the "aha moment"
- Have a live demo ready (practice it 5 times before the AMA)
- Record every AMA — clip the best moments for content

### 4. Engagement Strategy

**Who to follow and engage with:**
- @colosseum (Colosseum)
- @solana (Solana Foundation)
- @supaboringclub (Superteam)
- Top builders in your track
- Judges for your hackathon
- Ecosystem projects you integrate with

**How to engage:**
- **Reply with value** — don't just say "great post!" Add a technical insight or question
- **Quote-tweet with your take** — "This is exactly why we built [project]"
- **Join conversations early** — be one of the first replies on trending threads
- **Tag relevant people** — but only when genuinely relevant, not for clout

**Daily engagement routine (15 min):**
1. Check Solana trending topics
2. Reply to 3-5 relevant threads with genuine insights
3. Quote-tweet 1 post with your project's perspective
4. Like/retweet 5 posts from ecosystem projects
